## Ingredients

- 1 lb **Pollock Filets**, cut into 2-inch chunks
- 3 large **Russet Potatoes**, peeled and diced small (1/2 inch)
- 1 can (13.5 oz) **Coconut Milk** (Full Fat)
- 2 cups **Seafood Stock** (or Vegetable/Chicken broth)
- 3 stalks **Celery**, diced
- 1 **White Onion**, diced
- 1 cup **Corn** (Frozen or Canned)
- 1 teaspoon **Old Bay Seasoning** (or Paprika/Thyme mix)
- **Salt** and **Pepper**

## For the Side

- 1 lb **Brussels Sprouts**, halved
- **Olive Oil** and **Salt**

---

## Instructions

1.  **Roast Side:** Preheat oven to 400¬∞F. Toss Brussels sprouts with oil and salt. Roast for 20-25 minutes until crispy. (Do this while soup cooks).
2.  **Base:** In a soup pot, saut√© onion and celery in a little oil until soft (5 mins).
3.  **Simmer Potatoes:** Add the stock, potatoes, and Old Bay. Bring to a boil and cook for 10-12 minutes until potatoes are just tender.
4.  **Creamy Finish:** Pour in the coconut milk and corn. Bring to a gentle simmer.
5.  **Poach Fish:** Gently lay the Pollock chunks into the hot soup. Cover and turn off the heat (or keep on very low). Let sit for 5-7 minutes. The residual heat will cook the fish perfectly without breaking it apart.
6.  **Serve:** Ladle gently to keep fish chunks intact. Serve with the crispy Brussels sprouts on the side.
